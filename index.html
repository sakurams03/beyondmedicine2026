Biology Careers Across STEM

Vision and Mission

Vision: To empower the next generation to discover innovative and diverse careers where Biology meets Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics.

Mission: To provide clear, engaging, and accessible information about biology-related careers across Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics, while showcasing real world opportunities, and helping students make informed decisions beyond traditional medical pathways.

About Us

Biology Careers Across STEM is designed to help students discover the wide range of exciting career paths biology offers beyond traditional medicine. By challenging the common belief that biology only leads to becoming a doctor, this platform provides clear and engaging insights into real world opportunities across Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics, highlighting essential skills and guiding students to explore how biology connects to all areas of STEM.

Biology is more than just medicine; it’s the key to countless exciting careers in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math. Let's explore how biology can shape your future in ways you never imagined.

Biology+Science Field

As a microbiologist, you will study microscopic organisms such as bacteria, viruses, archaea, fungi and protozoa. This research includes investigation into their biochemistry, physiology, cell biology, ecology, evolution and clinical aspects, including the host response to these agents.

Marine biologists study life in the oceans and other saltwater environments. Their work ranges from observing the behavior of large marine mammals to researching the chemical properties of microscopic plankton, helping to protect marine ecosystems and biodiversity.

Biochemists explore the chemical processes and substances that occur within living organisms. By combining biology and chemistry, they investigate how molecules like proteins and DNA interact to carry out essential life functions, leading to breakthroughs in medicine and agriculture.

Zoologists are biologists who study animals and their interactions with ecosystems. They research animal behavior, physiology, evolution, and conservation, working to protect endangered species and maintain healthy wildlife populations.

Paleontologists study fossils to understand the history of life on Earth. They examine the remains of plants, animals, and other organisms to learn about their evolution, ecology, and the environments they lived in millions of years ago, helping us understand the Earth's past and predict its future.

Geneticists study genes, genetic variation, and heredity in organisms. They investigate how traits are passed down through generations and research the genetic basis of diseases, contributing to personalized medicine and genetic engineering.

Forensic scientists apply scientific principles to solve crimes. They analyze physical evidence such as DNA, fingerprints, and ballistics collected from crime scenes, providing crucial information for legal investigations and court proceedings.

Botanists study plant life, from the smallest mosses to the largest trees. They research plant physiology, ecology, and genetics, helping us understand how plants support life on Earth and developing sustainable agricultural practices.
